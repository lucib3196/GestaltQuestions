To determine the thermal efficiency and net work output for the Caffeinated Engine, we will utilize the concepts of the Rankine Cycle. The thermal efficiency of a Rankine Cycle can be expressed using the formula: $$ \eta_{thermal} = 1 - \frac{Q_{out}}{Q_{in}} $$ In this cycle:
  • $ Q_{in} = \dot{m} \cdot h_1 $ - heat added in the boiler.
  • $ Q_{out} = \dot{m} \cdot h_2 $ - heat rejected during condensation.
 For our cycle, we can deduce that: $$ h_1 = 3115 \, \text{kJ/kg} \quad \text{(at } P_1 = {{params.P1}} \, {{params.unitsPressure}}, T_1 = {{params.T1}} \, {{params.unitsTemperature}} \text{)} $$ $$ h_2 = 2350 \, \text{kJ/kg} \quad \text{(at } P_2 = {{params.P2}} \, {{params.unitsPressure}}) $$ Substituting these values into our efficiency formula: $$ \eta_{thermal} = 1 - \frac{ h_2 }{ h_1 } = 1 - \frac{ 2350 }{ 3115 } $$ Therefore, the thermal efficiency will be: $$ \eta_{thermal} \approx 0.245 \text{ or } 24.5\% $$ Now, we calculate the net work output: $$ W_{net} = Q_{in} - Q_{out} = \dot{m} \cdot (h_1 - h_2) $$ Substituting in the mass flow rate and enthalpy values, we find: $$ W_{net} = {{params.Mf}} \, \text{kg/s} \cdot (3115 - 2350) \, \text{kJ/kg} $$ Calculating this gives: $$ W_{net} = {{params.Mf}} \, \text{kg/s} \cdot 765 \, \text{kJ/kg} = {{params.Mf}} \cdot 765 \, \text{kW} $$ Hence, the net work output per cycle will be: $$ W_{net} \approx 76.5 \, \text{kW} $$
